Research part 2: Nutrition Nutrition: College students around the ages of 18 usually need 2200 calories (for women) and 2500- 3000 calories (for men) every day. A person’s calorie necessities largely depend on the person’s age, build, activity level, and other variables, so the “average” calorie intake should be verified by a nutritionist or doctor if possible.

The 3 musketeers of nutrients: Proteins, Carbohydrates, and Fat Carbs and Fat shouldn’t be avoided at all, in fact half of one’s calories comes from Carbs alone! There are good and bad sources of fat and carbohydrates, and this misconception that all of them should be avoided is very harmful.

The 3 main types of carbs are sugars, fibers, and starches and these can either be complex or simple. Usually complex carbs are considered “healthier” because they usually take longer to break down as well as having more important nutrients for the body. Fats also have a misconception, monounsaturated and polyunsaturated fats are usually healthy while saturated fats are unhealthy.
