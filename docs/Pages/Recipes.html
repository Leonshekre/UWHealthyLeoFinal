Staple ingredients

Making food for oneself in College isn’t as difficult as it seems. There are many ways to make your own food healthy, quick, and cheap without having to waste money on a dining plan. Most of them can also be done in less than 30 minutes!

The golden pantry staples: salt, pepper, cooking/vegetable oil, measuring cup, spices, and red (or white) vinegar. These common items can add the final pizzaz to a multitude of meals.

The magic of a rice cooker!

Rice is an incredible food that can be filling and healthy... is that it? No!
A rice cooker doesn't cook just "rice", it has the versatility to make rice, steamed vegetables, beans/lentils, quinoa, curry, and more!

Recipe x:Rice with lentils and vegetables!

Disclaimer: This is a personal recipe, measurements are not exact

Ingredients: vegetable oil, garlic powder, organic lentils, salt, white jasmine rice, water (will be boiled), and frozen vegetables (also a rice cooker)

    Pour 1/8 of a cup of vegetable oil into the rice cooker
    1 tbpsn garlic powder (or 2 shakes) into rice cooker
    Add 2 cups of rice
    Add 3 cups of water
    Pour in 4 tbspns of salt
    Add 1 cup of frozen vegetables
    Push "white rice" on the cooker
    Wait until it turns itself off

Easy Cereals

The easiest way to eat breakfast without spending 8$ a day. Be careful, many cereals are sweets in disguise!

The most widely available, healthy, and most delicious cereals are the original “Kellogg's corn flakes”, “Cheerios”, and “Fiber One” cereals. They have low sugar, high fiber, high protein, and organic ingredients.
